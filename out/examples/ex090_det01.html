Céu is designed to be deterministic in the sense that a given program, when executed multiple times, should always yield the same outcome when the same sequence of input events is repeated.

In this example, even though the variable v is accessed "concurrently" in the trails in parallel awaking to A, the execution is still deterministic.

The scheduler of Céu always executes the trails in the order they appear in the program.

This way, the first trail awakes first, assigns 1 and halts; then the second trail awakes and assigns 2. The final result is always 2.
