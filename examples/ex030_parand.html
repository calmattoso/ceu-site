If only a single line of execution were allowed, a program could not wait for multiple events at the same time, and Céu would be the most worthless language in the World.

A line of execution is known as a trail, and Céu allows multiple trails to coexist through parallel compositions.

Céu supports three kinds of parallel statements: par/and, par/or, and par. They differ in how they rejoin the trails on termination.

The par/and stands for parallel and, meaning that the trails spawned in parallel rejoin after they both terminate.

In the example, after the par/and rejoins, the loop restarts, printing the "Hello World!" message continuously.
